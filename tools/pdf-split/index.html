When Do You Need to Split a PDF?

Real-world scenarios where extracting pages from a PDF is essential.

Extracting Specific Chapters or Sections

Large PDFs like textbooks, research papers, or lengthy reports often contain more information than you need for a specific task. Splitting lets you extract just the relevant chapter or section, making it easier to share, annotate, or print only what matters — without wasting paper or sending a massive file via email.

Separating Scanned Documents

When you scan multiple documents at once, they often end up as a single PDF. Our split tool lets you separate each document into its own file, which is essential for organizing records, filing paperwork, or uploading individual documents to different online portals that have page-count or file-size restrictions.

Removing Sensitive Pages

Sometimes you need to share a PDF but certain pages contain sensitive or irrelevant information. Rather than editing the PDF, you can split out only the pages you want to share. Since all processing happens in your browser, even your sensitive documents remain completely private — nothing is ever uploaded to a server.
