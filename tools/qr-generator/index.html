Creative Ways to Use QR Codes

QR codes bridge the physical and digital worlds. Here are common ways to use them.

For Business and Marketing

If you run a small business, a restaurant, or an event, QR codes are essential. You can print a QR code on your business cards linking to your portfolio or LinkedIn profile. Restaurants commonly use them on tables to link directly to digital menus, providing a touchless experience for customers. You can also place them on flyers to link to a promotional video or a signup form.

For Wi-Fi Access

Instead of having guests constantly ask for your long, complicated Wi-Fi password, you can generate a QR code containing your network details. Guests simply scan the code with their smartphone camera, and they are instantly connected to your network without typing a thing.

How Do QR Codes Work?

A Quick Response (QR) code is a two-dimensional barcode that stores data as a series of pixels in a square grid. When a smartphone camera or scanner reads the grid, it decodes the pattern back into text, a web link, or an action (like sending an email or connecting to Wi-Fi). Our generator creates static QR codes, meaning the data is permanently encoded directly into the image pattern itself.
