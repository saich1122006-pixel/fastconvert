Why You Need Image Compression

Understanding file size requirements for online applications and exam forms.

Government Portal Photo Requirements

Many government portals and competitive exam registration forms in India have strict photo upload limits — typically under 20 KB or 50 KB for photographs, and under 50 KB to 100 KB for signature scans. Common portals that require compressed photos include UPSC, SSC, IBPS bank exams, NEET, JEE, state PSC applications, and passport applications. Failing to meet these requirements can prevent you from completing your application.

How Does Image Compression Work?

Image compression reduces file size by adjusting two factors: image dimensions (width and height in pixels) and quality level. Our tool intelligently scales down your image and adjusts the compression quality in multiple passes until the file size falls below your target — while keeping the image as sharp as possible. The output is always a JPG file, since JPG offers the best compression ratio for photographs.

Tips for Best Results

  • Use a passport-style photo — cropped headshots compress more efficiently than full-body or landscape images.
  • Start with a clear, well-lit photo — noisy or blurry images compress poorly and lose more quality at small sizes.
  • Check the portal's requirements carefully — some portals specify exact pixel dimensions (e.g., 200×230 pixels) in addition to file size limits.
  • Compare before and after — our tool shows you the original size, compressed size, and percentage saved so you can verify the result.
